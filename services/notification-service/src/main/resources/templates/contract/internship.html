3. POSITION AND LEARNING OUTCOMES

The internship is designed to enable the Intern to:

4. STIPEND

Monthly Stipend: R 8,000.00
Currency: ZAR
Payment Frequency: Monthly
Payment Method: Electronic transfer to the Intern's nominated bank account

5. WORKING HOURS

The Intern's working hours shall be 08:00 - 17:00, Monday to Friday, not exceeding 45 hours per week. The Intern shall not be required to work overtime except in exceptional circumstances and with prior agreement.

6. LEAVE

6.1 Annual Leave: The Intern is entitled to pro-rata annual leave in accordance with Section 20 of the BCEA.

6.2 Sick Leave: The Intern is entitled to sick leave in accordance with Section 22 of the BCEA.

6.3 Family Responsibility Leave: 3 days per annual cycle (BCEA S27).

7. MENTORSHIP AND EVALUATION

7.1 The Host Employer shall assign a designated mentor or supervisor to guide and support the Intern throughout the internship period.

7.2 The Intern's progress shall be evaluated at regular intervals (at minimum quarterly) against the learning objectives.

7.3 Upon successful completion of the internship, the Host Employer shall provide the Intern with a certificate of completion and a reference letter.

8. NO GUARANTEE OF EMPLOYMENT

8.1 This internship does not constitute a guarantee of permanent employment upon completion.

8.2 Should a suitable vacancy arise, the Intern may apply and will be considered alongside other candidates on merit.

9. TERMINATION

9.1 Either party may terminate this agreement by giving 2 (two) weeks' written notice.

9.2 The Host Employer may terminate the agreement immediately in cases of serious misconduct, including dishonesty, negligence, or breach of confidentiality.

10. CONFIDENTIALITY

The Intern agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all proprietary and business information encountered during the internship, both during and after the internship period.

11. DEDUCTIONS

Any deductions from the stipend shall only be made in accordance with Section 34 of the BCEA.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Disputes shall be resolved through the Host Employer's internal procedures and, if unresolved, may be referred to the CCMA.

13. GENERAL

13.1 This agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties.

13.2 Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

13.3 Governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa.
